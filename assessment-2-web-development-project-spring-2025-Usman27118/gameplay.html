Core Game Mechanics

The Drop

Choose your landing spot carefully as you parachute onto the island

Looting

Scavenge for weapons, armor, and supplies to increase your chances

The Zone

Stay inside the play zone as it shrinks over time

Winner Winner Chicken Dinner

Game Modes

Classic Battle Royale

The original PUBG experience. 100 players parachute onto an island, scavenge for weapons and equipment, and fight to be the last person standing. The playable area shrinks over time, directing surviving players into tighter areas.

  • Solo, Duo, and Squad options
  • First-person (FPP) and third-person (TPP) perspectives
  • Ranked and unranked matches

Arcade

Arcade Mode in PUBG (PlayerUnknown’s Battlegrounds) is a faster, more casual alternative to the traditional Battle Royale mode. It’s designed for players who want intense, short matches without spending too much time looting or waiting in long lobbies.

  • Arcade games are much shorter, usually lasting under 10 minutes.
  • Fights take place in tighter, action-focused areas for more frequent encounters.
  • Unranked matches

Team Deathmatch

Team Deathmatch (TDM) in PUBG is part of the Arcade Mode and is all about fast-paced gunfights without the need for looting or survival tactics. It’s ideal for players who want to focus purely on combat skills.

  • Practice with different weapons
  • Improve your reflexes and recoil control
  • Unranked matches

Pro Tips

Choose your landing spot based on the plane's path. High-risk, high-reward areas like military base have better loot but more players. Remote areas are safer but have worse loot.

Prioritize healing items (first aid kits, medkits), ammunition for your primary weapons, and throwables (grenades, smokes). Drop unnecessary attachments to save space.

Always be aware of the zone timer. Early game, you can loot longer. Late game, position is more important than loot. Use vehicles strategically but don't rely on them in final circles.
